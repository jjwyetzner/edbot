  1. Space out your studying: cramming the night before an exam is ineffective and can cause stress and anxiety. Spacing out your studying can help you cover more material and better prepare yourself for your exams.

  2. Mistakes are okay: everyone makes mistakes, but the important thing is what we learn from them. Don’t feel bad when you don’t perform as well as you hoped. Instead, focus on what you can improve for next time.

  3. Use interactive study methods: reading notes can help, but there are more interactive ways to study that help you learn better. Some of these ways include flashcards, games, and projects.

  4. Make and stick to a plan: creating a plan helps you understand what you still have to accomplish and helps you better manage your time.

  5. Study in a comfortable environment: the best place to study varies by person, but try to find a study space that is quiet, well-lit, and free of distractions. Some potential options are the library, a cafe, or at a friend’s house.
